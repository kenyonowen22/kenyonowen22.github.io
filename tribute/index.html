Jalen Brunson:New York's Savior

Jalen Brunson in action for the New York Knicks

Childhood and College

Jalen Brunson was born on August 31, 1996 in New Brunswick, New Jersey. From a young age, he had always been in love with basketball. His dad was in the NBA and now is a assitant coach on his sons team. Jalen always was a hard worker and eventually became a 4-time Illinios High School state champion. He commited to Villanova to play D1 college basketball. In his three years there, he won two national championships.

Early Career

In the 2018 NBA draft, Jalen was selected with the 33rd pick by the Dallas Mavericks. While it was extremely low for a player of his calliber, he finally had completed his dream. In his rookie year, he averaged 9.3 points, 3.2 assists and 2.3 rebounds in 73 games. A great start to his young career. He second year was about the same, he struglled with injuries and only played in 57 games. But he took a big leap in his 3rd year, averaging 12 points per game. He finished 4th in the 6th man of the year voting

Rise to Stardom

In the 2021-2022 season, Brunson blossemed into a star. He averged 16 points a game, became a starter, and was key in the Mavericks playoff run to the Western Conference finals. He played key minutes with Luka Doncic, their star, out, giving Dallas big playoff wins. In that offseason, he signed with the New York Knicks on a 4 year, $104 million dollar deal. Many people crtitzed this move at the time, saying Brunson will never lead the Knicks. Boy were they wrong.

Superstar

In the 2022-2023 season, Brunson took the Knicks to the second round of the playoffs for the first time in 10 years. He averaged 24 points and 6 assitsts, showing he really was the star for the Knicks. The next year, his impact exploded, averaging nearly 29 points and 7 points. The Knicks lost in 7 games in the second round again, but showing they are ready to make the next step. In the 2024-2025 season, Brunson averegd 26 points and 7 assists, and with tons of new pieces, the Knicks were poised to make a playoff run. Brunson won the clutch player of the year award, and the Knicks advanced the Eastern Conference Finals for the first time in 25 years. The Knicks lost in six, but one thing is for sure. The Knicks have found their guy.
